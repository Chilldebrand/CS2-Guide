# Aim, movement, and peeking

## The order of a good fight

1. Put the crosshair at head height.
2. Clear the nearest or most dangerous angle first.
3. Stop or counter-strafe.
4. Fire a controlled burst.
5. Reposition if the fight is not immediately favorable.

## Choose the peek for the job

- **Jiggle/shoulder peek:** collect an AWP or rifle reaction without committing.
- **Wide swing:** clear a close angle or punish an isolated defender; only do it when the next player can trade.
- **Crouch peek:** use sparingly; it changes your head level but slows the escape.
- **Flash peek:** throw or receive a flash before exposing yourself to two angles.

Do not clear five angles with one wide swing. Slice the pie, use cover, and let teammates hold the angles you have already cleared.

## Spacing

On attack, the second player should be close enough to see the first player’s fight but not so close that one spray kills both. On defense, avoid stacking two players in the same predictable line unless you are intentionally setting a crossfire.
