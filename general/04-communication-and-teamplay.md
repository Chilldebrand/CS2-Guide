# Communication and teamplay

## A good call has four parts

**Where · how many · what happened · what next.**

Example: “Two top mid, one tagged, smoke fading; I’m falling to connector.”

Use the map’s common callout, avoid invented names, and repeat the most important information once if teammates are fighting. Do not flood voice chat during a clutch.

## Team actions worth calling

- “Trade me.”
- “Hold my flash.”
- “I have flank; keep pressure.”
- “Save after this fight.”
- “One more, then hit.”
- “Do not fight; wait for utility.”

The goal is not to become an in-game coach. It is to make the next two seconds clearer for the teammate who can act.
